TheTalkList

Mobile Application

Introduction

TheTalkList is a real time video learning learning mobile platform. It connects you with individuals from all over the world so you can learn at your own schedule.

The Challenge

The platform's feedback form provided insight that many users thought the service was costly. The self reported age group of our users ranged from 16 to 23 years old and categorized themselves as students under the "I am a" field. So given this feedback, how do we keep our users engaged and valued?

My Role

Intially, I was brought on as a software developer where I was developing and maintaining features, but my role soon evolved as I saw a need for UX/UI design and research to make the application more intuitive.

The Discovery


Research

I created a survey asking 19 college students what incentivized them to return to a business or platform frequently. I received a majority of reasons including product satisfaction, rewards programs, and self improvement. Students wanted a "deal." These examples included Starbucks, Bing Rewards, and clothing reward systems. We did a competitve analysis of Starbucks and Bing Rewards to develop our design.

For TheTalkList, it is important to incorporate a rewards system that would make students feel like the more they go, the more they save.


User Testing

We tested our first low fidelty prototype and found that users responded well with the rewards program. However, the users did not want to see the breakdown of the point system.

Our design drew inspiration from other mobile rewards programs.
Our second iteration features collapsible information of the point system and users responded positively. However, we discovered some users were not clear with what they could redeem.
Our third iteration features redeem buttons next to each reward and is disabled to a gray when they do not have enough points to redeem. Our users were able to clearly identify what rewards they could redeem and identify the number of points needed to redeem a bigger reward.

The Deliverables

Our discoveries helped us create a new feature that would increase our number of active users on a monthly basis of seven percent.

When users do not have enough points, the redeem buttons will not be active. When users have enough points to redeem certain rewards, the redeem button will become active.

The Future

The Rewards feature was introduced in November 2018 and brought a seven percent increase, but the overall point icon could be improved to tell the user how many points are needed like Starbucks and other reward programs. In addition, the real estate can afford a feature to let the user know how many of each reward they can redeem quickly.
